About Dallin Overstreet

Professional Background

I am a PhD candidate at Arizona State University's School of Public Affairs, specializing in Public Administration & Policy. My research focuses on the intersection of public finance, economics, and policy analysis, with a particular emphasis on understanding complex social problems through rigorous econometric and causal inference methods.

Prior to pursuing my doctoral studies, I gained valuable experience as an analyst at the Arizona Department of Economic Security, where I developed practical insights into how policy decisions impact real-world outcomes. This experience informs my research approach and commitment to evidence-based policy solutions.

Research Philosophy

I believe in the power of data and rigorous analysis to solve complex social problems. My work combines theoretical foundations with empirical analysis to provide actionable insights for policymakers and public administrators. I'm particularly passionate about understanding how fiscal policies affect different communities and how we can design more effective and equitable public programs.

Research & Publications

Research Interests

My research focuses on several interconnected areas within public policy and administration:

  • Public Finance: Trust fund solvency, fiscal federalism, and intergovernmental finance
  • Public Economics: Labor market policies, minimum wage effects, and income inequality
  • Political Economy: Regulatory capture, barriers to entry, and policy diffusion
  • Unemployment Insurance: System design, trust fund management, and policy effectiveness
  • Causal Inference: Econometric methods for policy evaluation
